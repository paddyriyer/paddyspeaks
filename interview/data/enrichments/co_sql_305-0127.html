Sales reps don't all carry the same quota. Ranking by raw dollars rewards the rep with the biggest territory — not the most effective closer. To answer "who actually performed?" we move from raw ranking to Attainment Analytics: success measured as a percentage of each rep's specific goal.

1. The source data

Targets vary. A senior rep might have a $500k target, while a junior rep has a $100k target. Success = percentage of goal reached.

2. The SQL — attainment & rank

This query computes attainment percentage and ranks reps by success, not raw dollars.

WITH AttainmentData AS (
  SELECT
    rep_id,
    amount,
    target,
    -- What % of target was met (1.0 = exactly hit, >1.0 = over-attained)
    (amount * 1.0 / target) AS attainment_ratio
  FROM sales
)
SELECT
  rep_id,
  amount,
  target,
  ROUND(attainment_ratio * 100, 2)                                   AS attainment_pct,
  -- Hard yes/no on goal — gatekeeper for accelerators & President's Club
  CASE WHEN attainment_ratio >= 1 THEN 'YES' ELSE 'NO' END           AS met_target,
  -- 0.00 = worst attainment, 1.00 = best (ties take the lower position)
  PERCENT_RANK() OVER (ORDER BY attainment_ratio)                    AS success_rank,
  -- Quartile: 1 = at-risk · 2-3 = on-track · 4 = over-performer
  NTILE(4) OVER (ORDER BY attainment_ratio)                          AS success_quartile
FROM AttainmentData;

Tie-handling note: Reps B, C and E all attained 80% — PERCENT_RANK assigns ties the same lowest position (0.00). NTILE(4) distributes ties across adjacent quartiles deterministically (here by row order in the engine).

4. Strategic explanations

The "raw vs. relative" flip

Look at Rep C vs Rep D. Raw amount: Rep C ($400k) crushed Rep D ($120k). Attainment: Rep D is the winner at 120% of goal; Rep C is an underperformer at 80%. In most Sales Performance Management systems, Rep D earns a larger bonus despite the smaller absolute number — they're the most effective closer relative to expectations.

The "met target" boolean — your gatekeeper metric

The CASE creates a hard yes/no line. Even if a rep has a high PERCENT_RANK, if met_target = 'NO' they may not qualify for the President's Club or commission accelerators. This is the single most-asked column in any SPM dashboard review.

NTILE(4) instead of NTILE(100) — quartiles for action

Quartile 4 = over-performers (accelerated commission). Quartiles 2-3 = on-track (standard). Quartile 1 = at-risk (manager coaching plan). Coarser tiles map cleanly to operational decisions; percentiles are too granular to drive action.

Why attainment_ratio lives in a CTE

Computing the ratio once and reusing it three times (in attainment_pct, met_target, and the two window functions) keeps the math single-source. Inline it everywhere and a future engineer changes one occurrence and forgets the others — the dashboard silently drifts.

Senior signal: the interviewer wants to hear that "ranking by amount" is the wrong question. The right question is "ranking by relative performance" — and PERCENT_RANK + NTILE are the two functions that turn that into actionable commission tiers.
