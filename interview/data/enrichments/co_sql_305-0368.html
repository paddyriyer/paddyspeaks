Airbnb's cancellation rate varies dramatically by stay length: 1-2 nights have 8-12% cancel rate; 30+ nights have 2-3%. The query buckets by stay length and reports rate per band.

1. The source data — bookings

booking_idlisting_idnightscancelled
B_01L_0130

2. The SQL — CASE-bucketed cancellation rate

SELECT
  CASE
    WHEN nights <= 2  THEN '1-2 nights'
    WHEN nights <= 6  THEN '3-6 nights'
    WHEN nights <= 14 THEN '1-2 weeks'
    ELSE '15+ nights'
  END AS stay_band,
  COUNT(*) AS bookings,
  SUM(cancelled) AS cancellations,
  ROUND(100.0 * SUM(cancelled) / NULLIF(COUNT(*), 0), 1) AS cancel_rate_pct
FROM bookings
GROUP BY stay_band
ORDER BY cancel_rate_pct DESC;

3. Strategic explanations

Why bucket — not raw nights

Per-night cancel rates produce a noisy curve. Buckets compress them into 4 readable bands. The bucket boundaries are picked to match the business: weekend (1-2), weeklong (3-6), week+ (7-14), monthly (15+).

Cancel-by-whom — the missing dimension

"Cancelled" lumps host-cancels and guest-cancels. They have different costs:

  • Host cancel — host violates Airbnb's reliability promise, penalty applies
  • Guest cancel — refund varies by host's policy (flexible/moderate/strict)

Always split.

Time-to-cancel

Cancellations 7+ days before check-in are different from same-day. Same-day cancels disrupt the host more. Slice by lead-time:

CASE WHEN julianday(cancelled_at) - julianday(checkin_date) <= 1 THEN 'last_minute' END
The cancellation-policy interaction

Strict-policy listings see lower guest-cancel rate (less refundable, fewer change of mind). The same listing under flexible policy might see 2x cancels. Comparing across policies is unfair.

Cohort by booking lead-time

Bookings made 6 months ahead have more time to cancel; same-day bookings rarely cancel. Lead-time and stay-length confound. Senior analysis controls for both.

The "cancel and rebook" pattern

Some "cancellations" are actually upgrades — guest cancels short stay to rebook longer one. Production tracks these as modifications, not pure cancellations.

Senior signal: use CASE-bucketed bands for readability, split host-cancel vs guest-cancel for cost asymmetry, slice by time-to-cancel and cancellation-policy, and disambiguate cancel-and-rebook as modifications. Lead-time vs stay-length confound is the over-the-bar control.
