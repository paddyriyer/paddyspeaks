Spotify Family supports up to 6 members. Underutilized accounts (1-2 active members on a 6-seat plan) are downgrade candidates. The query computes utilization rate per account.

2. The SQL — utilization + per-member listening

SELECT fp.account_id, fp.max_members,
  COUNT(DISTINCT fl.member_id) AS active_members,
  ROUND(100.0 * COUNT(DISTINCT fl.member_id) / NULLIF(fp.max_members, 0), 1) AS utilization_pct,
  ROUND(SUM(fl.listened_minutes) * 1.0 / NULLIF(COUNT(DISTINCT fl.member_id), 0), 0) AS avg_minutes_per_member
FROM family_plans fp
LEFT JOIN family_listening fl ON fl.account_id = fp.account_id
GROUP BY fp.account_id, fp.max_members
ORDER BY utilization_pct DESC;

3. Strategic explanations

Why LEFT JOIN — keep zero-active accounts

Some accounts pay for Family but have 0 active sub-members. INNER JOIN drops them; LEFT JOIN keeps them with 0 active. Those are the highest-priority winback / downgrade-prevention targets.

The geo-fraud edge

Spotify Family requires same-household. Members in different countries flag the fraud system. The query above doesn't expose this; production joins to member_geo for the violation check.

Per-member share

"5 active members" can mean 5 equal-listen members OR 1 dominant + 4 minimal. The shape matters for "what would happen if one cancels"? Senior segmentation:

SUM(POWER(member_share, 2)) AS hhi -- Herfindahl concentration index
Senior signal: LEFT JOIN to keep zero-utilization accounts, segment by member-count for action, raise the household-geo fraud check, and use HHI for member-concentration ("dominant member" risk).
