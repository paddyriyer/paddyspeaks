Snap Discover is the editorial channel surface. The metric: subscriber-share of views — high subscriber-share signals loyal audience; low signals casual passers-by.

2. The SQL — subscriber vs casual breakout

SELECT channel_id,
  COUNT(*) AS views,
  COUNT(DISTINCT user_id) AS unique_viewers,
  SUM(is_subscriber) AS subscriber_views,
  ROUND(100.0 * SUM(is_subscriber) / NULLIF(COUNT(*), 0), 1) AS subscriber_share_pct
FROM discover_views GROUP BY channel_id
HAVING COUNT(*) >= 3
ORDER BY views DESC;

3. Strategic explanations

The subscriber-loyalty signal

Subscriber share > 60% = strong subscriber base, predictable audience. < 30% = relies on casual algorithmic surfacing — fragile to algorithm changes.

Convertible casual viewers

1-time casual viewer who returns becomes a subscriber. The "second-visit subscribe rate" is the conversion metric. The query above shows the OUTCOME; the funnel is upstream.

Time-decay

Channels viral last week then fade vs sustained channels — the second is the editorial bet. Restrict to a 30-day window for honest engagement signal.

Per-content-format split

Within a channel, different content types have different subscriber-share. News updates (subscriber-loyal) vs trending memes (casual-magnet). Slice by content_type.

Ad-rate impact

Channel ad-rate is set by subscriber-share — high-subscriber channels charge premium. The query result is part of the rate-card calculation.

Senior signal: subscriber share as loyalty signal, convertible-casual as the upstream funnel, time-decay for relevance, per-content-format split, and connect to ad-rate-card economics.
