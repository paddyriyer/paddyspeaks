A pivot that reports raw hours answers "how much"; a pivot that reports each genre's share of its category total answers "how much, relatively" — the question a reviewer actually asks. The share is a second stage layered on the pivot, and it hides two traps: integer division, and percentages that refuse to sum to 100.

1. The source data

The same categories tree and titles table as the pivot exercise. The leaf-genre totals: Superhero 180, Spy 60, Comedy 100 (all under Film); Drama Series 160, Reality 25 (under Series).

2. The query — pivot in a CTE, divide in the outer SELECT

WITH category_hierarchy AS (
  SELECT
    c1.category_id   AS root_id,
    c1.category_name AS root_name,
    COALESCE(c3.category_id,   c2.category_id,   c1.category_id)   AS descendant_id,
    COALESCE(c3.category_name, c2.category_name, c1.category_name) AS descendant_name
  FROM categories c1
  LEFT JOIN categories c2 ON c2.parent_id = c1.category_id
  LEFT JOIN categories c3 ON c3.parent_id = c2.category_id
  WHERE c1.parent_id IS NULL
),
pivoted AS (
  SELECT
    h.root_name AS main_category,
    COALESCE(SUM(t.watch_hours), 0) AS total_watch_hours,
    COALESCE(SUM(CASE WHEN h.descendant_name = 'Superhero'    THEN t.watch_hours END), 0) AS superhero_hours,
    COALESCE(SUM(CASE WHEN h.descendant_name = 'Spy'          THEN t.watch_hours END), 0) AS spy_hours,
    COALESCE(SUM(CASE WHEN h.descendant_name = 'Comedy'       THEN t.watch_hours END), 0) AS comedy_hours,
    COALESCE(SUM(CASE WHEN h.descendant_name = 'Drama Series' THEN t.watch_hours END), 0) AS drama_series_hours
  FROM category_hierarchy h
  LEFT JOIN titles t ON t.category_id = h.descendant_id
  GROUP BY h.root_id, h.root_name
)
SELECT
  main_category,
  total_watch_hours,
  ROUND(100.0 * superhero_hours    / NULLIF(total_watch_hours, 0), 1) AS superhero_pct,
  ROUND(100.0 * spy_hours          / NULLIF(total_watch_hours, 0), 1) AS spy_pct,
  ROUND(100.0 * comedy_hours       / NULLIF(total_watch_hours, 0), 1) AS comedy_pct,
  ROUND(100.0 * drama_series_hours / NULLIF(total_watch_hours, 0), 1) AS drama_series_pct
FROM pivoted
ORDER BY total_watch_hours DESC;

4. Strategic explanations

Why the pivot lives in a CTE

A SELECT cannot reference one of its own output aliases from a sibling expression — superhero_pct cannot read a superhero_hours defined in the same SELECT list. So stage one (pivoted) computes the conditional sums and names them; stage two reads those names and divides. The alternative — repeating every SUM(CASE WHEN ...) inside the division — works but is unreadable.

100.0, not 100 — the integer-division trap

100 * 180 / 340 in integer arithmetic truncates to 52. Writing the literal as 100.0 makes the whole expression numeric, so the division keeps its fractional part: 52.94..., which ROUND(..., 1) renders as 52.9. The decimal point on the literal is the entire fix.

NULLIF guards divide-by-zero

NULLIF(total_watch_hours, 0) returns NULL when the total is zero, and dividing by NULL yields NULL rather than raising an error. A root category with no titles produces a clean empty cell instead of crashing the query.

The trap: percentages that do not sum to 100

Series shows 86.5 for Drama Series and 0.0 for the other three — a total of 86.5%, not 100%. The missing 13.5% is the Reality genre: 25 of Series' 185 hours, counted in total_watch_hours but captured by no named column. Film, by contrast, sums to ~100 (52.9 + 17.6 + 29.4 = 99.9, the 0.1 lost to rounding) because its three named columns happen to cover all 340 of its hours.

Reading the gap correctly

When pivoted percentages do not reach 100, that is a signal worth a sentence in your answer: it is either rounding noise (a few tenths) or a genuine residual category outside the column list (a large, clean gap). Here it is the latter, and it is intentional — the report breaks out four genres and rolls everything else into the total only.

Senior signal: share-of-total over a pivot is two stages — aggregate, then divide — bridged by a CTE because a SELECT cannot see its own sibling aliases. Force numeric division with 100.0, guard zero denominators with NULLIF, and treat "doesn't sum to 100" as a question to answer, not a bug to hide.
