TikTok Live monetizes via virtual gifts (coins → diamonds → USD). Top streamers earn $10K+/stream. The query ranks streamers by gift volume and computes dollar value.

2. The SQL — per-streamer aggregate + rank

SELECT streamer_id,
  COUNT(*) AS gifts_received,
  COUNT(DISTINCT viewer_id) AS unique_gifters,
  SUM(gift_value_coins) AS total_coins,
  ROUND(SUM(gift_value_coins) / 100.0, 2) AS dollar_value,
  RANK() OVER (ORDER BY SUM(gift_value_coins) DESC) AS revenue_rank
FROM live_gifts GROUP BY streamer_id ORDER BY total_coins DESC;

3. Strategic explanations

The 100 coins = $1 ratio

TikTok's monetization layer: viewer pays $1 per 100 coins; streamer gets ~50% as diamonds; cash-out at $0.005/diamond. Net to streamer: ~25-50% of viewer spend.

Gift concentration

Pareto: top 1% of streamers earn 80% of gifts. RANK exposes the cliff.

Whale-driven economics

One viewer dropping a $500 "Universe" gift makes a stream's revenue. Per-stream variance is huge. Production tracks "median gifter contribution" alongside total to spot whale-dependence.

Watch-time → gift conversion

Streams with high watch-time but low gifts have monetization problem (audience there but not paying). Production joins watch + gift for the gap analysis.

Anti-fraud — gift returns

Some gifts are charged back / disputed. Net gift revenue subtracts these. The query gross; production net.

Senior signal: 100 coins = $1, ~25-50% net to streamer, Pareto concentration is extreme, watch-without-gift signals monetization gap, and net-of-chargeback for honest revenue.
