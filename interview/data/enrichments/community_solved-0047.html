A Community member poses a market-surveillance question: in a feed of trades, find pairs on the same stock that happened within a 10-second window and moved the price by more than 10%. That pattern hunts for a flash spike or a possible manipulation. The reference solution computes a price difference — but quietly forgets two of the three conditions in the question, and the interviewer is watching whether you catch it.

1. The source data — trade_tbl

Trades 1–3 are AAPL within an 8-second span; 4–5 are MSFT, ten minutes later. The genuine alerts are AAPL 1→2 (a 13% jump in 5s) and MSFT 4→5 (a 20% jump in 4s). Comparing trade 1 (AAPL) against trade 4 (MSFT) is meaningless — different stocks, ten minutes apart — yet that is exactly what the reference produces.

2. The SQL — self-join on stock + 10s window

SELECT a.trade_id AS trade_1, b.trade_id AS trade_2,
       a.trade_stock,
       ROUND(ABS((b.price - a.price) / a.price) * 100, 2) AS price_diff_pct
FROM trade_tbl a
JOIN trade_tbl b
  ON a.trade_stock = b.trade_stock
 AND a.trade_id < b.trade_id
 AND ABS(EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (b.trade_timestamp - a.trade_timestamp))) <= 10
WHERE ABS((b.price - a.price) / a.price) * 100 > 10
ORDER BY a.trade_id, b.trade_id;

This is the corrected query. The join enforces all three conditions the question states: a.trade_stock = b.trade_stock (same stock), the EXTRACT(EPOCH ...) term (within 10 seconds), and the WHERE (price difference over 10%). a.trade_id < b.trade_id keeps each pair once.

4. Strategic explanations

The bug — the reference dropped two of the three conditions

The original reference solution cross-joins every trade pair on t1.trade_id < t2.trade_id alone:

-- ORIGINAL (broken): no stock match, no time window
FROM trade_tbl t1 CROSS JOIN trade_tbl t2
WHERE t1.trade_id < t2.trade_id
... ABS((1.0*(price_2-price_1)/price_1)*100) >= 10

It never compares Trade_Stock and never touches Trade_Timestamp. So it pairs AAPL trade 1 with MSFT trade 4 — comparing the price of two different companies, ten minutes apart — and reports a "100% difference". That answers a question nobody asked. The title is explicit: "same stock", "range of 10 sec". A surveillance query that ignores both is not a stricter filter, it is wrong: it floods analysts with false positives and the real same-stock spikes drown in noise. The fix is to move both conditions into the join.

The INT cast also corrupts the number

A second flaw: the original wraps the percentage in CAST(... AS INT), truncating 13.33% to 13 and 11.18% to 11. For a threshold comparison at exactly 10% that truncation can flip an 10.6% move (a real alert) to 10 — or push a 9.8% non-event nowhere useful. Surveillance thresholds need the real fractional value; keep it numeric and ROUND only for display.

The asymmetry decision — trade_id < trade_id

a.trade_id < b.trade_id does two jobs: it stops a trade pairing with itself, and it emits each pair once rather than twice (1–2 and 2–1). It also gives a natural "earlier vs later" reading. Note this orders by trade_id, not timestamp — fine if ids are assigned in time order; if not, compare on trade_timestamp instead so "first" really means first.

The window-function alternative for adjacency

If the question meant only consecutive trades on a stock (not every pair within 10s), LAG is cheaper than a self-join:

SELECT * FROM (
  SELECT trade_id, trade_stock, price, trade_timestamp,
         LAG(price)          OVER w AS prev_price,
         LAG(trade_timestamp) OVER w AS prev_ts
  FROM trade_tbl
  WINDOW w AS (PARTITION BY trade_stock ORDER BY trade_timestamp)
) s
WHERE ABS((price - prev_price) / prev_price) > 0.10
  AND EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (trade_timestamp - prev_ts)) <= 10;

Clarify with the interviewer: any two trades within 10s (self-join), or strictly back-to-back trades (LAG)? They give different answers when three trades cluster.

The division-by-zero and direction edges

The denominator is a.price. A trade at price 0 (a bad tick, a halted symbol) would divide by zero — guard with a.price <> 0 or NULLIF(a.price, 0). Also note ABS(...) catches both a spike and a crash; if surveillance only cares about upward jumps, drop the ABS and test the signed ratio.

Senior signal: read every clause of the prompt — "same stock" and "within 10 seconds" are join conditions, not optional; never truncate a threshold metric with an INT cast; use trade_id < trade_id to make pairs single and directed; and guard the price denominator against zero.
