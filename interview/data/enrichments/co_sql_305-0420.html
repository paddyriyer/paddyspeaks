Reddit's "score" (upvotes - downvotes) is the engagement signal. Per-subreddit distribution exposes which communities reward which content. The query computes top/mean/min + decile rank.

2. The SQL — distribution stats per subreddit

SELECT subreddit_id,
  COUNT(*) AS posts,
  ROUND(AVG(score), 1) AS avg_score,
  MAX(score) AS top_score, MIN(score) AS min_score,
  NTILE(10) OVER (PARTITION BY subreddit_id ORDER BY score DESC) AS placeholder_decile
FROM posts GROUP BY subreddit_id, score ORDER BY subreddit_id, score DESC;

3. Strategic explanations

Reddit's Pareto distribution

1% of posts get 90% of upvotes. Per-subreddit shape varies: tightly-curated (askscience) has narrow distribution; loose (funny) has extreme Pareto.

Why per-subreddit — different cultures

r/askhistorians' "average score" of 50 is great. r/funny's average of 50 is terrible. Karma is community-relative; comparing across subs is meaningless.

Time-decay

3-year-old posts have accumulated more upvotes than 1-day posts. Reddit's algorithm uses time-normalized score:

(LOG(MAX(ABS(score), 1)) + (julianday(posted_at) - julianday('2005-12-08')) / 45000) AS hot_score
The downvote brigade

Negative scores ≈ "downvoted to oblivion". Filter to net-positive for "what's working":

WHERE score > 0
Awards layer

Reddit Gold/Premium awards add a paid signal beyond upvotes. Production weights awards 100x upvotes.

Senior signal: Pareto-distributed scores by community, normalize per-subreddit, use Reddit's hot-score time-decay, filter to net-positive for content quality, and weight awards heavily.
