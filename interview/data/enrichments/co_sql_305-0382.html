DoorDash's "Peak Pay" surge multipliers attract dashers during demand spikes. The query buckets orders by surge band and shows volume + revenue impact per band.

2. The SQL — banded surge analysis

SELECT market,
  CASE
    WHEN surge_multiplier < 1.3 THEN 'normal'
    WHEN surge_multiplier < 1.7 THEN 'mild'
    ELSE 'heavy'
  END AS surge_band,
  COUNT(*) AS orders,
  ROUND(AVG(fare), 2) AS avg_fare,
  ROUND(SUM(fare), 2) AS total_revenue
FROM orders GROUP BY market, surge_band
ORDER BY market, surge_band;

3. Strategic explanations

The supply-demand response

Heavy surge should attract drivers within 10 min, dropping back to mild. Sustained heavy surge for 30+ min = supply pipeline broken; needs operational intervention (Dasher recruitment campaigns).

Customer-side abandonment

Higher surge increases customer abandonment. Production tracks: at 1.7x surge, ~20% of carts abandon. Above 2x, ~40%. The optimal surge balances driver attraction vs customer loss.

Cuisine elasticity

Pizza customers are price-sensitive (abandon at 1.5x). Sushi customers less so (continue through 2x). Per-cuisine surge tuning is the modern approach.

Platform-vs-restaurant cut

Surge fees go to dashers (Peak Pay) or to DoorDash (service fee). Splitting which entity benefits informs pricing strategy.

Senior signal: use 3-band surge (normal/mild/heavy) with policy cutoffs, watch sustained-heavy as supply-pipeline failure, surface customer-abandonment elasticity, and split per-cuisine.
