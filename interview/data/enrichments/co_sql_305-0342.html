Google Analytics' canonical chart: the conversion funnel. Landing → Sign-up → Activate → Convert. The query computes per-step user counts and step-to-step conversion. The senior layer is the strict-vs-loose funnel distinction and cohort-vs-snapshot.

2. The SQL — counts + step-to-step + percent-of-top

WITH counts AS (
  SELECT step, COUNT(DISTINCT user_id) AS users
  FROM funnel GROUP BY step
)
SELECT step, users,
  LAG(users) OVER (ORDER BY step) AS prev_users,
  ROUND(100.0 * users * 1.0 / NULLIF(LAG(users) OVER (ORDER BY step), 0), 1) AS step_to_step_pct,
  ROUND(100.0 * users * 1.0 / FIRST_VALUE(users) OVER (ORDER BY step), 1) AS pct_of_top
FROM counts
ORDER BY step;

3. Strategic explanations

Step-to-step vs percent-of-top — both are needed
metricquestion it answers
step_to_step_pct"how leaky is THIS step?"
pct_of_top"of original visitors, who's still here?"

The two surface different problems: a step with 95% step-rate but the funnel converts 1% overall has 50 leaky earlier steps; a step with 30% step-rate is the single broken funnel step.

The strict-vs-loose funnel trap

The query treats anyone hitting step N as having "reached" step N. But did they hit steps 1..N-1 first? A user who deep-links into step 4 (skipping 2 and 3) shouldn't count toward step 4's conversion in a sequential funnel. The strict version requires a per-user CASE:

WITH per_user AS (
  SELECT user_id,
    MAX(CASE WHEN step = 1 THEN 1 END) AS hit_1,
    MAX(CASE WHEN step = 2 THEN 1 END) AS hit_2,
    MAX(CASE WHEN step = 3 THEN 1 END) AS hit_3,
    MAX(CASE WHEN step = 4 THEN 1 END) AS hit_4
  FROM funnel GROUP BY user_id
)
SELECT
  SUM(hit_1) AS step_1,
  SUM(CASE WHEN hit_1=1 AND hit_2=1 THEN 1 END) AS step_2,
  SUM(CASE WHEN hit_1=1 AND hit_2=1 AND hit_3=1 THEN 1 END) AS step_3,
  SUM(CASE WHEN hit_1=1 AND hit_2=1 AND hit_3=1 AND hit_4=1 THEN 1 END) AS step_4
FROM per_user;
The temporal-window addition

Production funnels add: "hit step N within 7 days of step 1". A user who lands on day 1 and converts on day 90 is technically converted but not from this campaign. Add timestamp checks to the strict funnel.

The cohort dimension

The PM's actual question is "is the funnel improving over time?" Slice by acquisition cohort and re-run the funnel per cohort:

WITH cohorts AS (
  SELECT user_id, DATE(MIN(reached_at)) AS cohort_date FROM funnel WHERE step=1 GROUP BY user_id
)
... join + GROUP BY cohort_date, step
FIRST_VALUE for the denominator

FIRST_VALUE(users) OVER (ORDER BY step) grabs step-1's count and replicates it on every row. Cleaner than a self-join to step 1.

The "leakiest step" diagnostic

Sort by step_to_step_pct ascending — the smallest number is the biggest leak. Production dashboards highlight that cell in red and trigger a redesign sprint.

Senior signal: emit both step-to-step and percent-of-top, surface the strict-vs-loose funnel distinction, layer the time-window predicate, and pivot to cohort-funnel for the "is it getting worse?" PM question. FIRST_VALUE for the top denominator is the cleaner-than-self-join pattern.
