Spotify's content team monitors skip rate as the inverse of listener satisfaction. Genres with high skip rate either have weak track curation or wrong-listener targeting. The query computes per-genre skip rate + average completion.

2. The SQL — skip rate + avg completion

SELECT genre,
  COUNT(*) AS plays,
  SUM(skipped) AS skips,
  ROUND(100.0 * SUM(skipped) / NULLIF(COUNT(*), 0), 1) AS skip_rate_pct,
  ROUND(AVG(played_seconds * 100.0 / NULLIF(song_seconds, 0)), 1) AS avg_completion_pct
FROM plays
GROUP BY genre
HAVING COUNT(*) >= 3
ORDER BY skip_rate_pct DESC;

3. Strategic explanations

The Spotify "skip" definition

Internally Spotify defines skip as "played < 30 seconds AND user moved to next track". The 30-second threshold is the royalty boundary — under 30s doesn't pay royalties. The query treats any flagged-as-skipped row uniformly; production differentiates.

Why average completion alongside skip rate

A track played 95% then skipped at the outro is different from one skipped at 5 seconds. Both register as skipped=1. Completion percentage exposes the difference. The genre with high skip rate AND low completion is broken; high skip rate but high completion means users are exit-browsing (next track) — that's normal.

The contextual confounder — playlist vs Discover

Skip rate is structurally higher on Discover Weekly (users are sampling) than on user's own playlists. Comparing across contexts is unfair. Always slice by play context (playlist / radio / discover / album).

Length-bias — long tracks skip more

An 8-minute track has higher skip rate than a 3-minute track simply because users have more chances to skip. Production normalizes by hazard rate (skips per minute of exposure).

The tie-back — algorithm feedback

Tracks with sustained high skip rate get demoted in autoplay queues; users who consistently skip a genre have it weighted-down in their recommendations. The query result is a feature, not just a dashboard.

Senior signal: distinguish 30-sec royalty-boundary skip from any-skip, surface completion as the second axis, isolate context (playlist vs Discover), and connect to the recommender feedback loop.
