Google Ads' core economic question: which match-type performs?. Exact match has high CTR but small reach; broad match has huge reach but unpredictable quality; phrase sits in between. The query computes spend, revenue, and ROAS per match-type.

1. The source data — ad_keywords

keyword_idmatch_typeimpressionsclickscostrevenue
K_001exact100050$25$200

2. The SQL — ROAS = revenue / spend

SELECT match_type,
  SUM(impressions) AS impressions,
  SUM(clicks)      AS clicks,
  ROUND(SUM(cost), 2)    AS spend,
  ROUND(SUM(revenue), 2) AS revenue,
  ROUND(SUM(revenue) * 1.0 / NULLIF(SUM(cost), 0), 2) AS roas
FROM ad_keywords
GROUP BY match_type
ORDER BY roas DESC;

3. Strategic explanations

ROAS vs ROI — different denominators

ROASrevenue / spend"return on ad spend" — must be > 1 to be profitable on revenue
ROI(revenue − cost − COGS) / costtrue profit return — accounts for product cost
CPAspend / conversionscost-per-acquisition — easiest to negotiate caps on

Marketing-org reports ROAS; Finance reports ROI. The query above does ROAS.

Why Broad Match isn't always worse

Despite lower per-keyword ROAS, Broad Match captures search-intent variations the team didn't think of — and Google's smart bidding has improved its quality. The actual diagnostic: Broad Match's ROAS within the marketer's portfolio. Some advertisers find Broad delivers 80% of profit at scale; others find it bleeds.

The ROAS-by-spend-tier breakdown

Average ROAS hides the long tail. Top 10% of keywords might ROAS at 15x; bottom 50% at 0.5x. Aggregate ROAS = 4x masks the distribution. Use NTILE or PERCENT_RANK to surface keywords below the breakeven threshold:

NTILE(10) OVER (ORDER BY revenue / NULLIF(cost, 0) DESC) AS roas_decile
The attribution window

"Revenue" depends on attribution window: 1-day click, 30-day click, 30-day view-through. Switching from 1-day to 30-day can double revenue. Always pin the window in the title of the dashboard.

The "low-spend high-ROAS" trap

A keyword with $5 spend and $500 revenue (ROAS 100x) is exciting — and statistically meaningless. Add a HAVING:

HAVING SUM(cost) >= 100   -- minimum spend threshold for ROAS to be meaningful
Incrementality — the senior reframe

ROAS measures revenue attributed to the ad. Incrementality measures revenue that wouldn't have happened without the ad — the right number for budget decisions. Most paid-search converts users who would've found you organically; true incremental ROAS is often 30-50% of attributed ROAS. Mention this — it's the senior reframe.

Senior signal: distinguish ROAS / ROI / CPA, place each match-type on the reach-vs-quality spectrum, decile-rank to expose the long tail, and pin the attribution window. The incrementality-vs-attributed ROAS reframe is the over-the-bar move.
