Snap Stories: a sequence of snaps. The swipe-through rate per snap signals where viewers drop off — content teams use this to optimize narrative pacing.

2. The SQL — per-story swipe rate

SELECT story_id,
  COUNT(*) AS impressions,
  SUM(viewed) AS views,
  SUM(swiped_through) AS swiped_away,
  ROUND(100.0 * SUM(swiped_through) / NULLIF(SUM(viewed), 0), 1) AS swipe_through_pct
FROM story_snaps GROUP BY story_id
HAVING SUM(viewed) >= 3
ORDER BY swipe_through_pct;

3. Strategic explanations

"Swipe through" — the friction signal

Auto-advance is normal. Active swipe-skip is the friction signal — viewer is not enjoying. Story sequences with sustained swipe rate > 30% need re-editing.

Sequence position decay

Snap 1: 0% swipe (just started). Snap 5: 15-25%. Snap 10: 40-60%. Decay is universal; the question is HOW STEEP. Slice by sequence position to find the cliff.

Creator-tier divergence

Top-tier celebrity stories: 5-10% swipe. Friend stories: 15-25%. Discover-channel: 30-40%. Different baselines per audience-relationship tier.

The "first-3-second" hook

Viewers swipe within the first 3 seconds if the snap doesn't grab. Production tracks "passed the 3-sec hook" as a separate metric.

Algorithm tie-in

High-swipe stories get demoted in the discovery feed. Creators monitor swipe rate as their feedback signal. The query result is creator-facing analytics.

Senior signal: distinguish auto-advance from active-swipe, surface position-decay as universal but variable, contrast creator-tier baselines, and connect to algorithm-feedback for creators.
