Uber's Driver Operations team segments drivers into earnings deciles to target the right incentive at the right pool. Top decile already maxed out — incentives waste. Bottom 3 deciles need basic activation. The middle 5 deciles are the pricing-sensitive zone where bonuses move the needle. NTILE(10) is the canonical operator.

1. The source data — driver_weeks

driver_idweekgrosstipsbonus
D_0012024-W01$1,200$80$50

2. The SQL — NTILE(10) over total earnings

SELECT driver_id, gross, tips, bonus, gross + tips + bonus AS total_earnings,
  NTILE(10) OVER (ORDER BY gross + tips + bonus DESC) AS earnings_decile
FROM driver_weeks
ORDER BY total_earnings DESC;

3. Strategic explanations

NTILE vs PERCENT_RANK vs CUME_DIST
functionoutputuse when
NTILE(N)1..N integer bucketneed exactly N equal-sized buckets
PERCENT_RANK0.0-1.0 continuous rankneed the relative position
CUME_DIST0.0-1.0 cumulative distributionneed % of rows below this value

NTILE is what Driver Operations wants — clean buckets for campaign targeting.

The "tied at the boundary" gotcha

NTILE distributes rows as evenly as possible. If 100 drivers have identical total_earnings, NTILE assigns the first 10 to bucket 1, next 10 to bucket 2, etc. — splitting tied values across buckets. For ties: add a deterministic tiebreaker like driver_id to avoid run-to-run instability.

The PARTITION BY follow-up

Per-city deciles separate the New York high earners from Bangalore high earners — each city gets its own internal ranking:

NTILE(10) OVER (PARTITION BY city ORDER BY total_earnings DESC)
The component breakdown

Decile 1 = $$$$. But split into gross/tips/bonus — different drivers max different components:

  • High gross + low tips: airport drivers (long routes, no in-cabin chat)
  • High tips + medium gross: late-night downtown (vibe drivers)
  • High bonus: quest-completers, gaming the system

Per-component decile reveals strategy.

Decile transition tracking

Decile 7 → decile 5 is a driver getting more efficient. Decile 5 → decile 8 is a driver disengaging. Track week-over-week transitions:

LAG(earnings_decile) OVER (PARTITION BY driver_id ORDER BY week) AS prev_decile
Senior signal: pick NTILE for clean buckets vs PERCENT_RANK for continuous rank, raise the tied-boundary gotcha, and add city-PARTITION for fair cross-market segments. The decile-transition LAG is the over-the-bar campaign-targeting signal.
