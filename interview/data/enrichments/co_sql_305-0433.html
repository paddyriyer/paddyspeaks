Shopify's "Abandoned Cart Email" feature is one of its highest-ROI features for merchants. The query measures recovery rate within a 7-day window.

2. The SQL — time-bounded recovery attribution

SELECT COUNT(*) AS abandoned_carts,
  COUNT(c.email_sent_at) AS emails_sent,
  COUNT(DISTINCT CASE WHEN o.customer_id IS NOT NULL THEN c.cart_id END) AS recovered,
  ROUND(100.0 * COUNT(DISTINCT CASE WHEN o.customer_id IS NOT NULL THEN c.cart_id END)
              / NULLIF(COUNT(c.email_sent_at), 0), 1) AS recovery_rate_pct
FROM cart_events c
LEFT JOIN orders o
  ON o.customer_id = c.customer_id
 AND julianday(o.ordered_at) >= julianday(c.email_sent_at)
 AND julianday(o.ordered_at) - julianday(c.email_sent_at) <= 7;

3. Strategic explanations

The 10-15% benchmark

Industry: 10-15% of cart-recovery emails lead to a purchase. Above 15% = excellent (good email subject + offer). Below 5% = email content broken or shopper segment cold.

The 7-day attribution

Customer might come back after 30 days but for unrelated reasons. 7-day window isolates email-attributed conversions.

The SAME-cart vs ANY-purchase question

Did they buy what was in the cart, OR anything? The query attributes any purchase. Strict: AND o.product_id = c.product_id.

Email vs SMS recovery

SMS recovery converts 2-3x higher than email but at higher unsubscribe rate. Production splits.

The discount-temptation lever

Recovery emails with discount codes convert 2x non-discount. But discount cannibalizes margin. Per-merchant tuning.

Senior signal: 10-15% benchmark, 7-day window for clean attribution, distinguish same-cart vs any-purchase strictness, contrast email vs SMS, and balance discount lift against margin cost.
