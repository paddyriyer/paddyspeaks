LinkedIn Recruiter's headline metric: InMail response rate. Different industries respond at very different rates — recruiters tune their outreach by industry response baselines.

2. The SQL — cross-table response rate

SELECT m.industry,
  COUNT(*) AS inmails,
  SUM(i.replied) AS replies,
  ROUND(100.0 * SUM(i.replied) / NULLIF(COUNT(*), 0), 1) AS response_rate_pct
FROM inmails i
JOIN members m ON m.member_id = i.recipient_id
GROUP BY m.industry
HAVING COUNT(*) >= 3
ORDER BY response_rate_pct DESC;

The InMail credit system

LinkedIn refunds InMail credits if the recipient replies within 90 days. So "responded" includes "any reply, even 'no thanks'". Distinguish positive-reply from any-reply for true engagement metric.

Subject-line A/B testing

Same industry, different subject lines yield 2-3x response variance. Production tracks per-subject-line response, feeding ML personalization.

Sender-recipient seniority match

Junior recruiters reaching out to VPs respond 3x less than peer-to-peer. The "seniority gap" is a dimension to control for. Add to GROUP BY.

The follow-up-message lift

Single InMail response rate ~20%. Sequence of 2 messages → 30%. Sequence of 3 → 38%. Diminishing returns; sequence-length is itself a tunable variable.
