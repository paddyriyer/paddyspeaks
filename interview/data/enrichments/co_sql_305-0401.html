Pinterest boards: collections of pins. Active boards (10+ pins, recent activity) are health signals. New users with empty boards predict churn.

2. The SQL — board age band × pin count

SELECT
  CASE
    WHEN CAST((julianday('2024-04-01') - julianday(b.created_at)) / 30 AS INTEGER) <= 1 THEN 'new (<= 1mo)'
    WHEN CAST((julianday('2024-04-01') - julianday(b.created_at)) / 30 AS INTEGER) <= 6 THEN '1-6 mo'
    WHEN CAST((julianday('2024-04-01') - julianday(b.created_at)) / 30 AS INTEGER) <= 12 THEN '6-12 mo'
    ELSE '12+ mo'
  END AS board_age_band,
  COUNT(DISTINCT b.board_id) AS boards,
  COUNT(bp.pin_id) AS total_pins,
  ROUND(COUNT(bp.pin_id) * 1.0 / NULLIF(COUNT(DISTINCT b.board_id), 0), 1) AS avg_pins_per_board
FROM boards b
LEFT JOIN board_pins bp ON bp.board_id = b.board_id
GROUP BY board_age_band
ORDER BY avg_pins_per_board DESC;

The "stuck at 5 pins" signal

Boards with 5 pins after 6 months — abandoned. That's the user-engagement red flag. Targeting users with high abandoned-board ratio for re-engagement campaigns.

The "topical-relevance" cousin

A 200-pin board on coherent topic ("modern kitchens") is high-quality. A 200-pin board with mixed topics is dump-style. Coherence is a different metric — production adds NLP-based topic-cohesion score.

The seasonality wave

Wedding boards spike Jan (engagement season). Holiday boards spike Oct-Dec. Per-category cohorting reveals which boards are seasonal.

Public vs secret boards

Secret boards (hidden from feed) have 1.5x more pins on average — less performance pressure. Production splits public vs secret.

Senior signal: use age-banded cohorts for growth-curve assessment, identify "stuck at 5" as engagement red flag, surface topic-cohesion as the quality dimension, and split public vs secret for accurate engagement read.
