The Trust & Safety team's daily question: "why are riders cancelling? why are drivers cancelling?". The two sides have orthogonal motivations and require different fixes. The query is GROUP BY with a window-function denominator.

1. The source data — cancellations

ride_idcancelled_byreason
R_001riderchange_plans
R_002drivertraffic
R_003riderdriver_late

2. The SQL — per-side share of cancellations

SELECT cancelled_by, reason,
  COUNT(*) AS cancellations,
  ROUND(100.0 * COUNT(*)
              / SUM(COUNT(*)) OVER (PARTITION BY cancelled_by), 1) AS pct_of_side
FROM cancellations
GROUP BY cancelled_by, reason
ORDER BY cancelled_by, cancellations DESC;

3. Strategic explanations

The SUM(COUNT(*)) OVER PARTITION idiom

Inner COUNT(*) is the GROUP BY aggregate (per cancelled_by, reason). Outer SUM(...) OVER (PARTITION BY cancelled_by) sums those counts within the side — giving each row's denominator. One pass, no subquery.

Why split by side — not aggregate

"Top cancel reason: change_plans" tells the product team nothing — that's a rider-only signal. "Top driver cancel reason: traffic" is a routing-engine signal. Lumping the two erases the diagnostic value. Always split policy-relevant categorical splits at the same level as the action they drive.

Reason-text quality — the upstream pipeline issue

If reason is free-text from a UI dropdown, you'll see "other" dominate at 40%+. Production fix: enforce a controlled vocabulary upstream + back-fill "other" by routing a sample to a human-tagger. The query result with "other" > 30% is itself a data-quality alert.

Time-of-day cut

Cancel reasons skew heavily by daypart: traffic at 5pm rush, driver_late at 11pm sparse-supply, change_plans uniformly. Add hour to GROUP BY to identify the highest-impact intervention windows.

The financial impact follow-up

Each cancellation has a cost — driver time wasted, fee charged or refunded, NPS hit. Join to cancellation_fees to weight reasons by dollars, not just counts. A low-frequency-but-high-cost reason (e.g. safety_concern) might outrank "change_plans" on financial-impact view.

Senior signal: split by responsible side, name the SUM-of-COUNT-OVER-PARTITION idiom, raise the controlled-vocabulary data-quality concern, and weight by dollar impact for the financial review.
