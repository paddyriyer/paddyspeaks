Apple's App Store keeps 15-30% of in-app purchase (IAP) revenue. The query computes per-category gross + dev cut + Apple cut + share-of-total, with the share calculated as a window-aggregate.

1. The source data — iap_transactions

2. The SQL — gross + cuts + share

SELECT category,
  COUNT(*) AS transactions,
  ROUND(SUM(amount), 2) AS gross_revenue,
  ROUND(SUM(amount) * 0.85, 2) AS dev_revenue,
  ROUND(SUM(amount) * 0.15, 2) AS apple_cut,
  ROUND(100.0 * SUM(amount) / SUM(SUM(amount)) OVER (), 1) AS share_of_total_pct
FROM iap_transactions
GROUP BY category
ORDER BY gross_revenue DESC;

3. Strategic explanations

The 85/15 split — the simplification

Apple's actual cut varies: 15% for first $1M of dev annual revenue (Small Business Program), 30% above that, 15% for subscriptions in year 2+. The query uses 15% for clarity. Production joins to app_pricing_tier for the right per-app rate.

The aggregate-of-aggregate share

SUM(SUM(amount)) OVER () — inner SUM is the GROUP BY; outer SUM-window aggregates across all groups. One pass for the share computation.

The currency conversion gap

iap_transactions store local currency. Reporting in USD requires the at-purchase FX rate, locked at txn_time. Production:

JOIN dim_fx_rate fx ON fx.from_currency = t.local_currency
  AND fx.effective_from <= t.txn_at AND fx.effective_to > t.txn_at
SELECT SUM(t.amount * fx.rate_to_usd)
Refunds — net not gross

The query reports gross. Apple's net (refunds back to user) is 5-15% lower depending on category (impulse-buy categories higher refund). Subtract refunds for the financially-honest number.

Subscription vs one-time IAP

Different revenue dynamics: subs grow MRR; one-time spikes. The query treats both as equivalent. Production splits.

Senior signal: note the 85/15 simplification and look up tier-specific rates, use SUM-of-SUM-OVER for share, FX-rate-at-event-time, and split subs vs one-time IAP. Refund subtraction for net is the financial-correctness move.
