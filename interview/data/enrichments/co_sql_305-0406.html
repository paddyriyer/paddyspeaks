The marketing-to-sales-to-revenue waterfall — MQL (marketing qualified) → SQL (sales qualified) → Won. Each gate compounds; the worst gate is the highest-leverage fix.

2. The SQL — three-step funnel per quarter

SELECT substr(mql_at, 1, 7) AS quarter_proxy,
  COUNT(*) AS mqls, COUNT(sql_at) AS sqls, COUNT(won_at) AS won,
  ROUND(100.0 * COUNT(sql_at) / NULLIF(COUNT(*), 0), 1) AS mql_to_sql_pct,
  ROUND(100.0 * COUNT(won_at) / NULLIF(COUNT(sql_at), 0), 1) AS sql_to_won_pct,
  ROUND(100.0 * COUNT(won_at) / NULLIF(COUNT(*), 0), 1) AS overall_pct
FROM opportunities GROUP BY substr(mql_at, 1, 7) ORDER BY quarter_proxy;

3. Strategic explanations

The compounding gates

MQL→SQL 50% × SQL→Won 30% = MQL→Won 15%. Improving sql_to_won by 5pp doubles overall (15%→20%). Improving mql_to_sql by 5pp moves overall less (15%→16.5%). The leverage is at the worst gate.

Per-cohort tracking

Quarterly cohort variance shows whether gates are improving. A cohort with 60% mql→sql vs prior 45% = something changed (better lead-scoring? new SDR team?).

The "MQL → recycled" trap

Some MQLs get sent back to nurture (not yet ready). They eventually become SQL months later, distorting the cohort. Production tracks "MQL → SQL within 30 days" for cleaner cohorts.

The benchmark range

SaaS B2B: MQL→SQL 25-40%, SQL→Won 20-30%, MQL→Won 5-15%. Below these = funnel investment opportunity.

Senior signal: identify the worst-gate as highest-leverage, isolate cohorts for trend detection, restrict to 30-day MQL→SQL window for cleaner attribution, and cite benchmark ranges.
