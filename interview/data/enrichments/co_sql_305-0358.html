Stripe's "auth rate" — the % of payment attempts that successfully charge — is the single most-watched product number. The query slices by payment method and country, exposing the cells where authorization is failing.

1. The source data — payments

payment_idmethodcountrystatusamount
P_001cardUSsucceeded$50

2. The SQL — two-axis success-rate matrix

SELECT method, country,
  COUNT(*) AS attempts,
  SUM(CASE WHEN status = 'succeeded' THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) AS successes,
  ROUND(100.0 * SUM(CASE WHEN status = 'succeeded' THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)
              / NULLIF(COUNT(*), 0), 1) AS success_rate_pct
FROM payments
GROUP BY method, country
HAVING COUNT(*) >= 1
ORDER BY method, success_rate_pct DESC;

3. Strategic explanations

The matrix view

Single dimension (just country, just method) hides the cross-effect. Card payments in Brazil have 70% auth; card payments in US have 92% — totally different stories per country. Cross-tab is the actionable view.

The fail-reason breakdown

"Failed" is one bucket; production splits into:

  • insufficient_funds — user issue, retry later
  • card_declined — bank issue, contact bank
  • do_not_honor — bank silent fail, often network glitch
  • 3ds_required — needs second-factor authentication
  • fraudulent — Stripe's risk score blocked it

Each bucket has different remediation. Show the top-5 fail reason per cell.

The 3DS gate

EU's PSD2 mandates 3D-Secure for many transactions, dropping auth rate by 5-15pp. Comparing EU to US auth rate without controlling for 3DS is unfair. Slice by requires_3ds.

Network-level signal

Visa, Mastercard, Amex have different auth profiles per country. Grouping all card-method together hides the network split. Add card_network dimension.

The retry pattern

One "failed" attempt may be retried successfully 5 minutes later. Net auth rate (any of N attempts succeeded) is much higher than per-attempt rate. Production tracks both.

Senior signal: always cross-tab method × country, split fail reasons for remediation, isolate 3DS impact for EU comparison, separate card networks, and contrast attempt-rate vs net-rate. The EU 3DS-mandate effect is the over-the-bar regulatory framing.
