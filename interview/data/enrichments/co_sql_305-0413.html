TikTok's For-You-Page is constantly A/B tested. The query compares treatment vs control on watch-time — the canonical relevance metric.

2. The SQL — per-bucket aggregates

SELECT bucket, COUNT(*) AS views, COUNT(DISTINCT user_id) AS unique_users,
  ROUND(AVG(watched_seconds), 1) AS avg_watch_seconds,
  ROUND(SUM(watched_seconds) * 1.0 / NULLIF(COUNT(DISTINCT user_id), 0), 1) AS seconds_per_user
FROM fyp_experiments GROUP BY bucket ORDER BY bucket;

3. Strategic explanations

Why seconds-per-user, not seconds-per-view

Per-view inflates: a treatment that shows BIGGER videos can win on per-view without serving more total content. Per-user normalizes — total time spent on platform is the actual goal.

Statistical significance

2% lift on a 1B-user test = real. 2% lift on a 1K-user test = noise. Always pair with a CI:

1.96 * SQRT(VARIANCE(watched_seconds) / COUNT(*)) AS ci_seconds
The novelty effect

New ranker: users explore (high engagement). Plateau by week 2. A 7-day test overstates lift. Production runs 21+ day tests.

Heterogeneous treatment effects

Avg lift hides that treatment helped power users (+5%) but hurt new users (-3%). Slice by user-tenure.

The exploration-table tie-in

5% of impressions are random. Treatment lift = (treatment − exploration) / exploration. Without exploration, you measure attribution-not-causation.

Senior signal: seconds-per-user as the platform-level KPI, attach CI for stat-sig, allow novelty-decay window (21+ days), check heterogeneous effects by tenure, and reference the exploration table for causal lift.
