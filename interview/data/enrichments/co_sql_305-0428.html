Dashboard usage is the leading indicator of customer engagement. High views + many unique viewers = embedded into the workflow.

2. The SQL — views + unique viewers + active days

SELECT dashboard_id, COUNT(*) AS views, COUNT(DISTINCT user_id) AS unique_viewers,
  ROUND(COUNT(*) * 1.0 / NULLIF(COUNT(DISTINCT user_id), 0), 1) AS views_per_user,
  COUNT(DISTINCT DATE(viewed_at)) AS active_days
FROM dashboard_views GROUP BY dashboard_id HAVING COUNT(*) >= 3
ORDER BY unique_viewers DESC;

3. Strategic explanations

The reach-vs-depth axis

10 viewers × 100 views = depth (one team's daily dashboard). 100 viewers × 10 views = reach (cross-team reference). Both healthy patterns; very different content strategies.

Auto-refresh vs human view

Dashboards on a wall-mounted TV refresh every 30 seconds — millions of "views" but no engagement. Production tracks "interactive views" (clicks, time-on-page) separately.

Stale dashboards

Dashboard with 0 views in 30 days = abandoned. Production warns the owner; auto-archives if 90 days no views. Keeps the workspace clean.

The viral dashboard

Sometimes a dashboard "goes viral" inside a customer org — viewers grow 10x in a week. That's worth amplifying as a "Featured" template for similar customers.

The retention tie-in

Customers with > 5 actively-used dashboards have 3x renewal rate. Dashboard usage feeds the renewal model.

Senior signal: distinguish reach (many users) from depth (per-user view count), filter auto-refresh from interactive, archive abandoned dashboards, identify viral dashboards for templating, and connect to renewal prediction.
