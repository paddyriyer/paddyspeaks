Amazon's Prime team tracks the trial → paid → renewal funnel as the leading indicator of subscriber LTV. The query is a single SELECT with three COUNT conversions chained — uses the COUNT-of-non-NULL idiom.

1. The source data — prime_subs

user_idtrial_startedtrial_convertedfirst_renewalcancelled_at
U_001Apr 1May 1Jun 1NULL

2. The SQL — chained COUNT-of-non-NULL

SELECT
  COUNT(*) AS trials,
  COUNT(trial_converted) AS converted,
  COUNT(first_renewal)   AS renewed,
  ROUND(100.0 * COUNT(trial_converted) / NULLIF(COUNT(*), 0), 1) AS trial_to_paid_pct,
  ROUND(100.0 * COUNT(first_renewal)   / NULLIF(COUNT(trial_converted), 0), 1) AS paid_to_renew_pct,
  ROUND(100.0 * COUNT(first_renewal)   / NULLIF(COUNT(*), 0), 1) AS overall_renewal_pct
FROM prime_subs;

3. Strategic explanations

The COUNT(col) idiom

COUNT(col) counts non-NULL values. Replaces SUM(CASE WHEN col IS NOT NULL THEN 1 END). Cleaner for sequential funnel gates.

The compounding gates

Trial→Paid 80% × Paid→Renew 70% = Overall 56%. Track all three so you know which gate is leakiest. Trial→Paid 80% with overall 30% means renewal is collapsing.

The cohort dimension

Add cohort_week to GROUP BY to see if 80% trial→paid is consistent OR drifting:

GROUP BY substr(trial_started, 1, 7) AS cohort_month
Net vs gross renewal

"Renewed" should exclude users who cancelled BEFORE the renewal kicked in. Production form:

COUNT(CASE WHEN first_renewal IS NOT NULL AND (cancelled_at IS NULL OR cancelled_at > first_renewal) THEN 1 END)
The in-flight cohort hide

Cohorts that signed up < 1 month ago haven't had a chance to convert — their conversion rate is artificially 0%. Filter:

WHERE julianday(trial_started) <= julianday('now') - 30
Senior signal: the COUNT-of-non-NULL idiom for sequential funnels, hide in-flight cohorts, distinguish gross vs net renewal, and split by cohort to detect drift.
