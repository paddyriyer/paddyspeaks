Datadog tiered plans (Pro / Enterprise) come with quota limits. The query computes per-tier average utilization and identifies over-quota customers.

2. The SQL — utilization ratio per tier

SELECT plan_tier, COUNT(*) AS customers,
  ROUND(AVG(hosts_billed * 1.0 / NULLIF(hosts_quota, 0)), 2) AS avg_utilization_ratio,
  SUM(CASE WHEN hosts_billed > hosts_quota THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) AS over_quota_customers
FROM customer_usage GROUP BY plan_tier ORDER BY avg_utilization_ratio DESC;

3. Strategic explanations

Three customer states

< 30%oversold — downgrade candidate

> 95%imminent over-quota — sales call
Over-quota = revenue opportunity

Datadog charges overage at premium rate. Customers consistently > 100% are willing-to-pay-more candidates for upgrade negotiation.

The seasonal swing

Black Friday spike: e-commerce customers temporarily 200% utilization. Don't auto-upgrade; offer "burst pricing" instead.

Per-feature breakdown

Hosts quota is one. APM / RUM / Synthetics each have their own quotas. Customer might be 10% on hosts but 95% on APM. Production reports per-feature.

The renewal predictor

Utilization > 70% has 95% renewal rate. < 20% has 60% rate. The query feeds the customer-success book-of-business prioritization.

Senior signal: 4-tier utilization classification, identify over-quota as upsell, allow seasonal burst pricing, split by feature-quota not just hosts, and use utilization for renewal prediction.
