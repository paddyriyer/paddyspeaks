Subreddit growth velocity is one of Reddit's leading retention indicators. The query uses LAG over weekly subscriber snapshots to compute WoW change.

2. The SQL — LAG-based WoW growth

SELECT subreddit_id, week, subscribers,
  LAG(subscribers) OVER (PARTITION BY subreddit_id ORDER BY week) AS prev_week,
  subscribers - LAG(subscribers) OVER (PARTITION BY subreddit_id ORDER BY week) AS net_new,
  ROUND(100.0 * (subscribers - LAG(subscribers) OVER (PARTITION BY subreddit_id ORDER BY week))
              / NULLIF(LAG(subscribers) OVER (PARTITION BY subreddit_id ORDER BY week), 0), 1) AS wow_growth_pct
FROM subreddit_subs ORDER BY subreddit_id, week;

3. Strategic explanations

Net new vs % growth

r/news growing +10K/week (0.05% growth) is huge in absolute. r/newsmall growing +100/week (50% growth) is huge in relative. Both matter; show both.

The viral-event spike

A subreddit can gain 100K subs in a week from a viral event. Sustained 5%+ weekly growth = healthy organic. One-off spike = recheck next week.

Defaults and rebrands

Reddit's default subreddits get auto-subscriptions when users sign up. Their growth is mostly user-acquisition not community-quality. Filter out:

WHERE subreddit_id NOT IN (SELECT id FROM dim_subreddit WHERE is_default = 1)
The first-snapshot NULL

The earliest week per subreddit has prev_week NULL. Filter or COALESCE to 0.

Quitter detection

Some weeks show negative net_new (mass unsubscribe). Triggered by mod controversy, spam, or rebrands. Production alerts on -5% WoW for > 1 week.

Senior signal: emit both net-new and % growth, identify viral spikes vs sustained, exclude default subreddits from organic growth, handle the first-snapshot NULL, and alert on sustained negative growth.
