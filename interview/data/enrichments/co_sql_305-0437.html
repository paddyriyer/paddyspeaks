Coupon usage and AOV are intertwined. Comparing AOV with-vs-without discount reveals whether discounts grow basket size or just give away margin.

2. The SQL — two-bucket compare

SELECT has_discount, COUNT(*) AS orders,
  ROUND(AVG(total), 2) AS avg_order_value,
  ROUND(SUM(total), 2) AS total_revenue,
  ROUND(MIN(total), 2) AS min_order, ROUND(MAX(total), 2) AS max_order
FROM orders GROUP BY has_discount ORDER BY has_discount;

3. Strategic explanations

Discount AOV usually exceeds non-discount

Counterintuitive: discounted orders have HIGHER AOV. Customers add more items to "qualify" for the discount. The lift is real; the question is whether the margin loss is worth it.

The selection bias

Customers who use discounts are different from those who don't (more deal-conscious, larger baskets). Comparing them isn't a true experiment. Production runs randomized A/B for true causal estimate.

Margin impact

10% lift in AOV at 20% discount = net loss. Always pair with margin: SUM(total - cost - discount).

The discount-stacking problem

"Has discount" is binary; reality includes free-shipping, percent-off, BOGO, gift-with-purchase. Each has different elasticity. Production splits by discount-type.

The acquisition vs retention split

Discount on first order = acquisition tool. Discount on returning customer = unnecessary margin loss. Always split by customer-tenure.

Senior signal: AOV-with-discount usually higher (basket-building behavior), warn about selection bias, compute net of margin not just AOV, split discount-type for elasticity, and isolate first-order vs returning for true uplift.
