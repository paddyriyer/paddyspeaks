Sales coaching prioritizes the bottom-quartile reps. NTILE(4) over per-rep win-rate produces the segments. Top quartile = leave alone (let them sell). Bottom = coach intensively.

2. The SQL — per-rep win + quartile

WITH per_rep AS (
  SELECT owner_id, COUNT(*) AS opps,
    SUM(CASE WHEN status = 'won' THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) AS wins,
    ROUND(100.0 * SUM(CASE WHEN status = 'won' THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)
                / NULLIF(COUNT(*), 0), 1) AS win_rate_pct
  FROM opportunities GROUP BY owner_id HAVING COUNT(*) >= 3
)
SELECT owner_id, opps, wins, win_rate_pct,
  NTILE(4) OVER (ORDER BY win_rate_pct DESC) AS quartile
FROM per_rep ORDER BY win_rate_pct DESC;

3. Strategic explanations

Why HAVING ≥ 3 — sample size

A rep with 1 won deal has 100% win rate. With 3 deals, the rate is more reliable. Production threshold is 10+ deals for honest quartiles.

Win rate vs Win volume

50% × 4 deals = 2 wins. 25% × 100 deals = 25 wins. Same rep tier, very different scale. Production splits by quota size.

Lead-quality confound

A rep on government accounts has 10% win rate; a rep on SMB has 35%. Different markets. Stratify by territory or vertical for fair compare.

The bottom-quartile coaching loop

Bottom 25% reps get weekly 1:1s, deal reviews, ride-alongs. The query result drives the coaching scheduler. Tracking quartile transitions month-over-month measures coaching ROI.

Senior signal: threshold by sample size for stable rates, distinguish rate from volume, stratify by territory/vertical, and connect to coaching-loop interventions.
