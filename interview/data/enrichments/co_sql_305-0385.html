Tipping behavior is the single best signal of customer-side dasher economics. The query crosses order size × daypart for a tip-rate matrix.

2. The SQL — two-axis bucketed tip rate

SELECT
  CASE WHEN total < 25 THEN 'small' WHEN total < 60 THEN 'medium' ELSE 'large' END AS order_band,
  CASE
    WHEN CAST(strftime('%H', ordered_at) AS INTEGER) BETWEEN 17 AND 21 THEN 'dinner'
    WHEN CAST(strftime('%H', ordered_at) AS INTEGER) BETWEEN 11 AND 14 THEN 'lunch'
    ELSE 'offpeak'
  END AS daypart,
  COUNT(*) AS orders,
  ROUND(AVG(tip), 2) AS avg_tip,
  ROUND(100.0 * AVG(tip * 1.0 / NULLIF(total, 0)), 1) AS tip_pct_of_total
FROM orders GROUP BY order_band, daypart
ORDER BY order_band, daypart;

The tip-as-% pattern

% tip stays remarkably constant 10-18% across order sizes. Dollar amount scales with order. Predicts dasher income better than tip count.

The pre-tip vs post-tip dispatch

DoorDash shows dashers the pre-tip total when offering a job. Drivers cherry-pick high-pre-tip orders. Production tracks "pre-tip vs post-tip dispatch acceptance" — when tips are hidden, acceptance distribution evens out but average earnings differ.

The geographic tip culture

NYC tips average 22%; Texas 18%; Bay Area 15%. Per-market tip-rate informs region-specific Peak Pay calculations.

No-tip orders

10-15% of DoorDash orders have $0 tip. These are often rejected by dashers, creating wait-time problems. Filter:

WHERE tip = 0  -- isolate the rejected-by-economics segment
Senior signal: tip-as-% is constant 10-18% across order size, contrast pre-tip vs post-tip dispatch behavior, surface geographic tip-culture variance, and isolate $0-tip orders as the dispatch-friction segment.
