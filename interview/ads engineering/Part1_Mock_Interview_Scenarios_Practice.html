Billed revenue
Owner: Finance
What advertisers have been invoiced for. Produced by the billing system — at StreamCo, the Salesforce ledger. Lags reality by the billing cycle — typically a month. Excludes anything invoicing hasn't swept yet.
Updates: month-end + 3-day settlement.
Spend revenue
Owner: Ads pipeline
Sum of auction-clearing prices × served impressions, in the advertiser's contracted currency, converted to USD at the delivery-time FX. Produced near-real-time by the impression pipeline. Knows about impressions the billing system doesn't yet know about. Doesn't know about refunds or chargebacks.
Updates: streaming, ~5-minute lag.
Attributed revenue
Owner: Ads measurement
Revenue credited to specific campaigns under an attribution model — last-click 7-day, last-touch, MTA linear, whatever the advertiser's contract calls for. Depends on the window, the touch model, and the identity graph. Almost always differs from spend revenue because it excludes unattributable spend and absorbs post-attribution adjustments.
Updates: nightly batch for 28-day; streaming for 24-hour.
Settled revenue
Owner: Finance
The number Finance closes the month on. Post-refund, post-chargeback, post-IVT-recovery credits, post-partner-attribution-lag, post-currency-revaluation. Sourced from the ledger after all adjustments have posted. This is what the 10-Q filing is built from.
Updates: monthly, with quarter-end revisions.

Your Turn · SQL · 15 minutes on your timer

Write the reconciliation before reading the answer

The tables you have access to — full column-level catalog below so the query is unambiguous.

silver.fact_impression
one row per served impression · partitioned by date_trunc('hour', impression_ts) · ~2B rows/day
columntypewhat it is · why it is hereexample
spend_cents BIGINT Auction-clearing price in cents, in the advertiser's contract currency. Stored in cents to avoid floating-point drift on sums across billions of rows. In a second-price auction this is (second-bid + 0.01), not the winning bid. 247
advertiser_id BIGINT StreamCo advertiser account id. FK to dim.advertiser. Same id space as finance.ad_revenue_ledger — the two tables join cleanly on this column. 98421
campaign_id BIGINT Campaign id inside the advertiser. FK to dim.campaign. Multiple campaigns per advertiser per month; do not aggregate up without advertiser_id or you double-count across shared budgets. 5512980
impression_ts TIMESTAMP (UTC) Wall-clock time the impression was served. Stored in UTC. Finance's "September" is PST, not UTC — the reconciliation query has to respect that or you pull three extra hours of Oct 1 into the September bucket. Sub-second precision is not reliable; do not use for strict ordering. 2025-09-15 14:32:07
is_valid BOOLEAN FALSE iff the pre-bid filter flagged the impression as Invalid Traffic (bot, datacenter, repeat-user-cap breach). Catches ~94% of IVT. The remaining ~6% is caught post-impression by a third-party fraud vendor and appears later as ivt_credits_usd on the Finance ledger — that delta is a first-class part of the September gap. TRUE
currency VARCHAR(3) ISO-4217 code of spend_cents. Advertiser-contract currency, not the viewer's currency. 31 distinct currencies active at StreamCo; always check for NULL in ad-hoc queries — legacy rows from before 2024 can still surface. USD, EUR, JPY, INR
fx_rate_to_usd NUMERIC(12,6) FX rate at impression_ts to convert spend_cents into USD. Snapshotted at event time, not looked up at query time — so last week's dashboard reproduces exactly even if today's FX has drifted. Identity for USD rows is 1.000000 (never 1.0 — the precision matters for sum rounding). 1.087400
How spend_revenue is computed: SUM(spend_cents × fx_rate_to_usd) / 100.0 filtered by impression_ts in the target PST month. Gotchas: (1) divide by 100, not 100.0, and the result is an integer in some engines — always cast; (2) filtering on is_valid = FALSE is how you compute pipeline-flagged IVT, not how you exclude IVT from spend — spend includes the flagged rows and the downstream attribution step is where exclusion happens.
finance.ad_revenue_ledger
one row per (advertiser_id, month) · PST-aligned month · owned by Finance, not Data · month column stores first-of-month as DATE
columntypewhat it is · why it is hereexample
month DATE First day of the Finance close month, PST-aligned. Always first-of-month at midnight. Never trust a query that filters this column with a range — = is the correct operator. 2025-09-01
advertiser_id BIGINT FK to dim.advertiser. Same id space as silver.fact_impression. 98421
invoiced_usd NUMERIC(18,4) Amount invoiced to the advertiser this month, in USD at invoice-date FX. Lags impressions by the billing cycle — typically 30 days. This is "billed revenue" in Act 2's four-definitions list. 142,800.0000
refunds_usd NUMERIC(18,4) Credits issued back to advertisers for overcounted delivery, dispute, or pacing overshoot. Stored as a positive value; subtracted when computing settled. 3,200.0000
ivt_credits_usd NUMERIC(18,4) Finance-side IVT credits from the third-party fraud vendor that catches what the pre-bid filter missed. Stored as a positive value; subtracted when computing settled. Compare against pipeline-flagged IVT (sum of spend_cents × fx_rate where is_valid = FALSE) to explain the September IVT delta (~$160K at current volumes). 18,400.0000
partner_attribution_adjustment_usd NUMERIC(18,4) Programmatic-partner postback adjustment. Partner SSPs report final attribution on a 14-day lag, so September's settled revenue can only be finalised mid-October. Stored as a positive value; added when computing settled. This is the single largest contributor to the September gap (~$740K). 74,000.0000
fx_revaluation_usd NUMERIC(18,4) End-of-month FX revaluation for advertiser contracts held in non-USD. Can be positive or negative. Small in high-USD months (few bps), material in months with emerging-currency volatility (can move 2-5% of the contract). Added (with sign) when computing settled. +140.0000
How settled_revenue is computed (Finance's close formula):
invoiced_usd − refunds_usd − ivt_credits_usd + partner_attribution_adjustment_usd + fx_revaluation_usd
Gotchas: (1) sign conventions differ by company — at StreamCo refunds and ivt_credits are stored positive and subtracted; do not copy this formula to a different ledger without checking; (2) fx_revaluation_usd is signed — do not wrap it in ABS(), that loses a real ±2% swing in emerging-markets months.

Write one query that reconciles September. The output should be one row with all four revenue concepts, the per-category gap, and an unexplained_gap_usd. The last column is the one Raj is actually testing — if it is non-zero, you have a bug in the pipeline, and you need to be able to say so. If it's within rounding, the numbers are all explained by known adjustments, and you close the ticket.

Reference solution · try the prompt above first, then check against this
WITH pipeline AS (
  SELECT
    SUM(spend_cents * fx_rate_to_usd) / 100.0
      AS spend_revenue_usd,
    SUM(CASE WHEN is_valid = FALSE
             THEN spend_cents * fx_rate_to_usd ELSE 0 END) / 100.0
      AS pipeline_ivt_flagged_usd
  FROM silver.fact_impression
  WHERE impression_ts >= '2025-09-01'
    AND impression_ts <  '2025-10-01'
),
ledger AS (
  SELECT
    SUM(invoiced_usd)                        AS billed_revenue_usd,
    SUM(refunds_usd)                         AS finance_refunds_usd,
    SUM(ivt_credits_usd)                     AS finance_ivt_credits_usd,
    SUM(partner_attribution_adjustment_usd)  AS partner_adj_usd,
    SUM(fx_revaluation_usd)                  AS fx_reval_usd,
    SUM(invoiced_usd) - SUM(refunds_usd)
      - SUM(ivt_credits_usd)
      + SUM(partner_attribution_adjustment_usd)
      + SUM(fx_revaluation_usd)              AS settled_revenue_usd
  FROM finance.ad_revenue_ledger
  WHERE month = '2025-09-01'
)
SELECT
  p.spend_revenue_usd,
  l.billed_revenue_usd,
  l.settled_revenue_usd,
  -- per-category components of the gap
  p.pipeline_ivt_flagged_usd,
  l.finance_ivt_credits_usd,
  l.finance_refunds_usd,
  l.partner_adj_usd,
  l.fx_reval_usd,
  -- the unexplained residual
  l.settled_revenue_usd - p.spend_revenue_usd
    + (p.pipeline_ivt_flagged_usd - l.finance_ivt_credits_usd)
    + l.finance_refunds_usd
    - l.partner_adj_usd
    - l.fx_reval_usd                          AS unexplained_gap_usd
FROM pipeline p CROSS JOIN ledger l;

What the query returns today:

  • spend_revenue_usd = $8.21M — matches the dashboard exactly
  • settled_revenue_usd = $9.12M — matches Finance
  • partner_adj_usd = $0.74M — the partner-attribution lag, explained
  • finance_ivt_credits_usd − pipeline_ivt_flagged_usd = $0.16M — Finance's IVT catch exceeds our pre-bid filter, explained
  • fx_reval_usd = $0.02M — end-of-month revaluation, explained
  • unexplained_gap_usd = −$0.01M — within rounding. No pipeline bug.

The $900K gap decomposes cleanly. Neither side is wrong. Finance's number is more complete by construction. Yours is more timely by construction.

The Landmine · Currency math at monthly close

The $20K FX revaluation you almost missed

Your pipeline books revenue at the impression-time FX rate — that is what you charge the advertiser. Finance books the same revenue at the settlement-time FX rate, because that is what actually hits the bank. For most advertisers at most times, the gap is rounding. At quarter-end, when emerging-market currencies move, the gap can be six figures.

If you do not know this and you report unexplained_gap_usd = $20K, Raj assumes the pipeline has a bug. If you do know this and the column you named is fx_reval_usd, Raj knows you have shipped this system before.

Every adjustment line on the ledger exists because a real edge case happened at a real company. A senior engineer's job is to know which lines exist and why — before the reconciliation starts.

Rule: when you reconcile, you name every known adjustment category first. The residual is what's left after all the known ones are accounted for. Never call the gross difference the "gap."
