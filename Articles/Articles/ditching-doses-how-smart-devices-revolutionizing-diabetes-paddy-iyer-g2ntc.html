Part 1: From Pills to Pumps — Unveiling the Business Case

  1. Business Questions

Understanding the Business Requirements/Process for Switching from Pills to Wearable Insulin Devices.

Here are the specific business needs and functionalities involved in a diabetic patient transitioning from metformin to a wearable insulin device. Here’s what to consider:

1. Patient Journey:

  • Initiation: What triggers the initial discussion about switching medications? (e.g., uncontrolled blood sugar levels, complications, patient preference)

  • Evaluation: How does the healthcare provider assess the patient’s suitability for a wearable insulin device? (e.g., medical history, lifestyle factors, tech literacy)

  • Device Selection: What factors influence the choice of a specific wearable insulin device? (e.g., insurance coverage, features, user-friendliness)

  • Training & Onboarding: How is the patient educated and trained on using the device and mobile app?

  • Monitoring & Adjustments: How are blood sugar levels monitored during the transition, and how are insulin doses adjusted?

  • Feedback & Support: What mechanisms are in place for continuous feedback and support from the healthcare provider?

2. Key Stakeholders:

  • Patients: What are their needs, concerns, and expectations throughout the transition?

  • Healthcare Providers: What are their responsibilities in managing the transition and patient follow-up?

  • Device Manufacturers: What information and support do they offer to patients and healthcare providers?

  • Insurance Companies: What are their coverage policies for wearable insulin devices and associated apps?

3. Business Functionalities:

  • Device-Patient Interaction: How does the patient interact with the device to deliver insulin doses?

  • Mobile App Integration: What functionalities does the app offer for monitoring, data visualization, and dose management?

  • Data Communication & Security: How is patient data securely collected, transmitted, and stored?

  • Alerts & Notifications: What alerts and notifications are generated for both patients and healthcare providers?

  • Reporting & Analytics: What reports and insights are available to track patient progress and improve care delivery?

4. Key Challenges:

  • Patient Adherence: How can patient engagement and adherence to the new treatment plan be ensured?

  • Data Interoperability: How can data from different devices and apps be seamlessly integrated and analyzed?

  • Privacy & Security: How can patient data be protected from unauthorized access and breaches?

  • Cost & Reimbursement: How can the affordability of wearable insulin devices and associated apps be addressed?

5. Success Metrics:

  • Improved blood sugar control

  • Reduced risk of complications

  • Increased patient satisfaction

  • Optimized healthcare provider workflow
