The Three Eras: What Actually Changed

To understand why everyone dropped their coffee, you need to understand what just shifted.

Not incrementally. Categorically.

Era 1: Pre-AI Computing (1990-2020)

"The Human Does Everything"

The model:

Business Analyst creating quarterly report:
  1. Gather data from Salesforce (2 hours)
  2. Clean in Excel (1.5 hours)
  3. Analyze with pivot tables (3 hours)
  4. Build PowerPoint deck (2 hours)
  5. Review and refine (1.5 hours)
Total: 10 hours of human labor
Characteristics:
  • Software was just a tool
  • Humans did all thinking AND execution
  • Speed limited by human typing/clicking
  • More work = hire more people
The bottleneck: Human time
Era 2: Pre-Agentic AI (2020-2024)
"The Human Has a Smart Assistant"
The model:
Same quarterly report:
  1. Gather data (same - 2 hours)
  2. Clean data (AI writes formulas - 20 minutes)
  3. Analyze (AI suggests approach - 1.5 hours)
  4. Build deck (AI drafts outlines - 1 hour)
  5. Review (same - 1.5 hours)
Total: 6.2 hours (40% faster)
Characteristics:
  • AI accelerated thinking
  • Humans still executed everything
  • Had to copy/paste AI suggestions
  • Productivity up 30-50%
The critical limitation: AI could say "Here's the SQL query" but you still had to run it.
The bottleneck: Human execution
This was comfortable. AI made you faster, but you were still essential.
