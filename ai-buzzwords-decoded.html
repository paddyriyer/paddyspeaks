1Concept

LLM ‚Äî Large Language Model

ü¶ú Think of it as: A supercharged autocomplete that's read every library on Earth

You know how your phone suggests the next word when you're typing? An LLM does that, but it's read millions of books, websites, and conversations first. So its "guesses" are really, really good.

It doesn't truly "understand" things the way you and I do. It's more like a parrot that's read every library on Earth ‚Äî it mimics human language patterns so well that it feels like intelligence. When you type "What's the capital of France?", it doesn't "know" Paris. It predicts that "Paris" is the most likely next word given everything it's learned.

‚ñ∂ LLM in action

Real-world examples: GPT-4 (OpenAI), Claude (Anthropic), Gemini (Google), Llama (Meta), and Mistral are all LLMs. Some are massive (hundreds of billions of parameters), while others are smaller ‚Äî called SLMs (Small Language Models) ‚Äî optimized for speed, privacy, or running on your phone.

2Concept

AI Assistants ‚Äî The Friendly Face

üöó Think of it as: If the LLM is the engine, the AI Assistant is the car you actually drive

ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, DeepSeek ‚Äî these are all AI Assistants powered by LLMs underneath. The assistant wraps the raw LLM in a user-friendly package. It adds safety filters, conversation memory, personality, and formatting.

Without the assistant layer, talking to a raw LLM would be like talking to a savant who has no social skills ‚Äî technically brilliant but hard to work with.

‚ñ∂ Raw LLM vs AI Assistant

3Concept

RAG ‚Äî Retrieval-Augmented Generation

üìñ Think of it as: An open-book exam ‚Äî the AI looks up answers in YOUR documents before responding

Here's the big problem with LLMs: they only know what they were trained on. Ask about your company's internal HR policy? They'll guess ‚Äî or worse, confidently make something up (this is called a "hallucination"). Ask about something that happened yesterday? No clue.

RAG is the solution. Instead of relying solely on memorized knowledge, RAG first retrieves relevant documents from your actual data, then feeds those to the LLM so it can generate an answer grounded in real, accurate, up-to-date information.

‚ñ∂ RAG Pipeline ‚Äî from question to grounded answer

Why it matters: RAG reduces hallucinations, keeps answers up-to-date, and lets you build AI assistants grounded in YOUR data ‚Äî without retraining the entire model. It's the backbone of every "chat with your documents" tool you've seen.

4Concept

AI Agents ‚Äî The Self-Driving Employee

üë®‚Äçüç≥ Think of it as: Not a chef who gives you recipes ‚Äî a chef who checks your fridge, shops, cooks, and texts your guests the dinner time

This is where things get genuinely exciting. LLMs answer questions. RAG makes those answers accurate. But AI Agents actually DO things. They don't just tell you "here's how to book a flight" ‚Äî they go ahead and book the flight.

An AI Agent is an LLM with superpowers: it can think, plan, decide which tools to use, take action, check if it worked, and adjust. It's like giving the LLM not just a brain, but hands, feet, and a to-do list.

‚ñ∂ Agent trip planner ‚Äî autonomous multi-step execution

5Concept

MCP ‚Äî Model Context Protocol

üîå Think of it as: USB-C for AI ‚Äî one universal plug that connects any AI to any tool

Now here's the problem: if your AI Agent needs to talk to your email, your database, your calendar, your CRM, and your file system ‚Äî someone has to write custom code connecting each one. That's messy, fragile, and doesn't scale.

MCP, developed and open-sourced by Anthropic, is a standard protocol ‚Äî a universal plug ‚Äî that lets any AI model connect to any tool through one consistent interface.

‚ñ∂ MCP ‚Äî One protocol, infinite connections

What MCP exposes to the AI: Tools ‚Äî callable functions to take actions (send email, query database). Resources ‚Äî read-only documents for reference (policy docs, FAQs). Prompt Templates ‚Äî reusable instructions guiding how the AI responds in specific situations.

6Concept

A2A ‚Äî Agent-to-Agent Protocol

üí¨ Think of it as: The group chat protocol for AI agents ‚Äî agents delegating tasks to each other

MCP is about one agent connecting to many tools. But what happens when you have multiple specialized agents that need to work together? That's A2A.

A2A (Agent-to-Agent), open-sourced by Google, lets AI agents talk directly to each other ‚Äî delegate tasks, share results, and collaborate.

‚ñ∂ A2A ‚Äî Agents collaborating on a content pipeline

The key insight: You don't have to choose between MCP and A2A. An agent can use MCP to access tools AND use A2A to talk with other agents. They're complementary.

7Concept

Low-Code AI ‚Äî Build Without Coding

üß© Think of it as: LEGO blocks for AI ‚Äî snap pieces together, no programming required

Here's the best part: you don't need to be a programmer to build all of this. Platforms like Zapier, Make, and n8n let you wire together LLMs, APIs, tools, and data sources through visual drag-and-drop workflows.

‚ñ∂ Low-code workflow ‚Äî Email ‚Üí AI ‚Üí Slack ‚Üí Spreadsheet. Zero code.

The Big Picture ‚Äî How It All Connects

Each concept builds on the previous one, like layers of a cake:

A2A ‚Äî Agents collaborate with each other
MCP ‚Äî Agents connect to any tool via universal protocol
AI AGENTS ‚Äî LLMs that can think, plan, and act
RAG ‚Äî Ground answers in your real data
AI ASSISTANTS ‚Äî Friendly interface layer
LLMs ‚Äî The foundation: pattern-matching language engines
LOW-CODE ‚Äî Wire it all together without programming
The AI stack isn't magic. It's a layered system where each piece solves one problem. Understanding the layers is the first step to building with them ‚Äî or knowing when someone's selling you snake oil.
