Three questions. Three investigations.
Zero SQL.

Watch an agentic model receive a question in English, investigate across your data substrate, and deliver a complete answer — with root cause, evidence, and action — in seconds.

Jensen Huang stood on stage at Morgan Stanley's TMT Conference and said something that should have stopped every BI vendor's heart: "There will be no software in the future that's not agentic. Every software company will become an agentic company. They're going to simultaneously use open models that they download themselves and fine tune." Not a prediction. A statement of architecture. Companies will download open models, tune them on their own data, and deploy agents that do productive work. The SaaS model — renting tools for humans to operate — is being replaced by what Huang calls selling outcomes.

Now follow that logic one layer down. If every company runs its own tuned intelligence, and that intelligence can reason, query, and act — what happens to the dashboard? What happens to the BI tool that exists only because a human needed a visual proxy for data they couldn't interrogate directly? The entire analytics stack — from SQL console to KPI tile — was built for an era where humans were the query engine. That era just got a termination date.

The dashboard was never the destination. It was a compromise for a world where intelligence was expensive and human attention was the bottleneck. Both constraints just evaporated.

What follows isn't a wish list. It's the vocabulary for the infrastructure that's already being built — at GTC, inside OpenClaw deployments, in every enterprise fine-tuning a Nemotron or Llama variant on their own domain data. These are the words the industry doesn't have yet. The moment you can name a thing, you can build it.

FIVE SCENES
01
The Monday Ritual Dies

From Dashboard Morning to the Living Brief

The VP of Sales used to open six tabs every Monday. Squint at trend lines. Copy numbers into slides. Guess at causation. Forty minutes to build a mental model of what happened last week. That ritual exists because BI tools sell tools, not outcomes.

When a company runs its own fine-tuned model on its CRM, pipeline, and revenue data — the model doesn't render a chart and wait for a human to interpret it. It composes a #LivingBrief overnight: a narrative of what changed, why, and what to do about it. This isn't AI-assisted analytics. This is the model doing the analyst's job while the analyst sleeps. The economics are brutal: an agentic brief costs tokens. A human analyst costs salary, latency, and interpretation error.

#LivingBrief— A self-composing narrative. The model's output is English, not a chart.
SaaS Era6 dashboards → 40 min → slides → guesswork
Agentic EraTuned model → overnight brief → 3 min → action
02
The Jira Ticket to Analytics Dies

From Query Queues to the Intent Cast

A PM wonders: "Are mobile-first users stickier?" In the SaaS era, that's a Jira ticket to the data team. Two-day turnaround. A decontextualized chart lands in Slack.

In the agentic era, the company's fine-tuned model already knows the schema, the business logic, the segmentation rules. The PM performs an #IntentCast — English in, investigation out. The model doesn't just run one query. It segments by cohort, controls for geography, checks pricing tiers, and responds in a #NarrativePane — a conversational surface where the follow-up question morphs the chart in real time. This is what Huang means by "selling outcomes, not tools." The PM didn't use software. The software used itself.

#IntentCast— You throw intent. The tuned model catches meaning.
#NarrativePane— The conversation is the analysis. No tool, no tab, no ticket.
SaaS EraJira → analyst → 2 days → static chart
Agentic EraIntent Cast → tuned model → 10 sec → narrated insight
03
The Unseen Anomaly Speaks

From Threshold Alerts to the Signal Stream

Here's the architectural problem with dashboards: they only show what someone already thought to measure. A human wrote the alert rule. A human set the threshold. The dashboard answers known questions — it never generates the question you forgot to ask.

An agentic model fine-tuned on your operational data doesn't wait for thresholds. It runs continuous inference — Huang's "tokens per watt" measured as vigilance. The #SignalStream monitors the entire surface area of your data and surfaces #AwareMetrics — metrics that detect their own anomalies, diagnose probable cause by cross-referencing correlated signals, and retrieve historical precedent. Not "churn is up." Instead: "Unusual churn cluster in Brazil — 3.2σ — correlates with payment gateway latency spike 48h ago. Last time this pattern appeared, it resolved in 5 days." That's not an alert. That's an investigation.

#SignalStream— Continuous inference as surveillance. The model never blinks.
#AwareMetric— Self-diagnosing. A metric that ships with its own root-cause analysis.
SaaS Era"Churn > 5%" → shrug → investigate manually
Agentic EraCause + correlation + precedent + recommended action
04
The Board Deck Rewrites Itself

From Stale Slides to the Intelligence Surface

The CFO's board deck takes a week because five teams operate five disconnected tools: Finance pulls actuals from one system, FP&A models in another, Strategy writes commentary in a third. By presentation day, the numbers are already stale. This is the cost of software that sells tools — every tool requires a human operator, and every handoff introduces latency and error.

When the company deploys a model fine-tuned on its financial data, connected to live actuals, the #IntelligenceSurface replaces the entire assembly line. #SignalCards — self-contained units that carry their own explanation, trend, and recommended action — auto-generate for every KPI. The narrative layer rewrites itself when actuals change at 2 AM. The model even suggests which slides to cut based on what the board actually engaged with last quarter. This is what "Agent as a Service" looks like for finance: the CFO's job shifts from assembly to judgment.

#IntelligenceSurface— Live model inference renders data as awareness, not charts.
#SignalCard— Self-explaining. The KPI ships with its own paragraph.
SaaS Era5 teams → 5 tools → 7 days → stale on delivery
Agentic EraTuned model → live surface → auto-narrative → human judgment
05
The Relay Race Collapses

From Six Humans to One Intent — Ambient Awareness

Today, the journey from question to deliverable is a relay race: Stakeholder → PM → Analyst → BI Tool → Designer → Presenter. Six humans, four tools, three days. Each link in that chain is a SaaS product extracting a license fee for being operated by a human.

Huang's thesis kills this chain. When companies deploy agentic systems — fine-tuned models connected to their own data through platforms like OpenClaw and NemoClaw — the agent doesn't need a PM to translate the request, an analyst to write the query, or a BI tool to render the output. It is the PM, the analyst, and the BI tool. "Prepare me for the QBR" becomes a complete deliverable: data pulled, story identified, narrative drafted, slides built, talking points ready. This is #AmbientAwareness — intelligence always running, always connected, always ready. Not a tool you go to. An environment you exist in. The compute translates directly into intelligence, the intelligence translates into revenue. Huang said it plainly: "I'm certain compute equals revenues."

#AmbientAwareness— You don't seek data. The model is always on, the insight is always ready.
SaaS Era6 humans → 4 tools → 3 days → 1 deck
Agentic Era1 intent → tuned agent → 10 min → complete deliverable
THE ARCHITECTURE

The Stack Beneath the Lexicon

This is not magic. It has five layers.

Layer 1
Data Substrate
Warehouse / lakehouse + semantic layer + event streams + knowledge graph. This is the Delta Lake, Iceberg, Unity Catalog layer — the governed, versioned truth. Nothing above it works without lineage and access control here.
Layer 2
Business Memory
Metrics definitions, governance rules, access policies, historical decisions, audit trails. This is the institutional context the model needs to reason correctly — not just data, but the rules about the data.
Layer 3
Agentic Reasoning Layer
Fine-tuned / open models + tool use + retrieval + planning + verification. This is where Nemotron, Llama, Mistral get tuned on your domain data and connected to your systems via OpenClaw / NemoClaw. The model reasons, queries, cross-references, and verifies before it speaks.
Layer 4
Narrative Interface Layer
This is where the lexicon lives. Living Briefs, Signal Cards, Narrative Panes, Intent Casts — the surfaces through which intelligence reaches humans. English is the rendering engine. Charts become evidence, not product.
Layer 5
Action Loop
Ticket creation, slide generation, CRM updates, workflow triggers, forecast adjustments, escalation routing. The output isn't a report. It's work done. This is where "selling outcomes" becomes literal.
The dashboard was a rendering layer. The agentic system is a reasoning-and-action layer.

No, not everything disappears.

Regulated workflows, audit trails, financial controls, SOX compliance, and human sign-off will preserve visual surfaces for years. Operations centers, trading floors, and production monitoring genuinely benefit from spatial, glanceable, real-time displays. Those aren't going anywhere.

But the role changes. The chart stops being the product. It becomes evidence attached to an agentic conclusion.

The governance layer — lineage, access control, explainability — doesn't go away. It gets harder, because the consumer of the insight is now further from the source than ever. That's not a reason to keep dashboards. That's a reason to build better Layer 2 business memory.

The vocabulary exists now.
The infrastructure is being built.

This isn't futurism. The open models are shipping. The fine-tuning pipelines are operational. The agentic frameworks have crossed from research prototype to enterprise deployment. OpenClaw is, as Huang put it, the Linux of AI agents — and every company needs a strategy for it. The question is not whether the SaaS analytics stack gets absorbed into the agentic layer. The question is whether you're the one doing the absorbing, or the one being absorbed.

These twelve terms are a starting point — a language for builders, not spectators. Because the moment you call it a "dashboard," you've already limited what it can become. And the companies downloading Nemotron, Llama, and Mistral right now? They're not building dashboards. They're building intelligence.
