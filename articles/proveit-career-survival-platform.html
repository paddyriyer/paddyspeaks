LinkedIn's Core Model Is Dead

LinkedIn still runs on the most naive assumption in the modern internet: people will tell the truth about themselves. That assumption was shaky even in 2015. In the AI era, it's laughable.

Today, anyone can generate in minutes:

  • A resume that looks like a Stanford graduate wrote it
  • A portfolio with "case studies" that never happened
  • A job history full of fake companies
  • Certifications copied from templates
  • 30 posts a month written by an LLM
  • Recommendation letters that sound emotional and "authentic"

A credibility crisis disguised as networking.

‚ú¶ ‚ú¶ ‚ú¶

In 2026, Skills Must Be Proven, Not Claimed

Nobody cares if you wrote: "Expert in Python, AWS, GenAI, MLOps, Data Engineering." That sentence is now meaningless. Any random college kid can generate it in 10 seconds.

The future platform must force people to demonstrate:

  • Real projects
  • Real work artifacts
  • Real outcomes
  • Real proof
  • Real timelines
In the AI era, resumes are cheap. Proof is expensive. And expensive things are harder to fake.

The Proof Builders
